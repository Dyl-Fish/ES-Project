General Appearance

Animals

Temperate rainforests are rich in biodiversity, with a wide variety of unique animal species. Some of the most common animals in this ecosystem include the black bear, deer, elk, mountain lion, raccoon, and squirrels. These animals are well adapted to the moist vegetation and mild temperatures of the temperate rainforest, using the lush foliage to find food and shelter. The majority of the animals in this ecosystem are omnivores, feeding on both plants and other animals. Some species, like the black bear, rely heavily on salmon and other fish that live in the rivers and streams of the temperate rainforest. These animals play an important role in maintaining the delicate balance of the ecosystem, as they help distribute nutrients and control the population of other species.
