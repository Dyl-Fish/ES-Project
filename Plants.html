The general plantlife in the Temperate Rainforest

The population of a temperate rainforest typically includes many signature plants, full with lush greens and earthy tones, the temperate rainforest is home to few types, but many plants. The plantlife in this ecosystem includes tons of slippery moss that grows nearby streams and on damp soil where the wester redcedar trees emerge alongside the thickening ferns of varying sizes. The temperate rainforest has a wide variety of plant life that can be seen throughout the canopy of this ecosystem. Most of the plants lower to the ground are popular among the animal life of the ecosystem. The plants in this ecosystem are 100s of years old and the trees can range fromm 100-250 ft in height.
